Corrective Assumption 1 · The 100M Household Discrepancy

Narrative says ~100M. Appendix 1 sums to 130M. We reconcile — explicitly.

Raw Appendix 1 top-two tiers: 45M + 85M = 130M ≠ "nearly 100M" stated in the narrative.

The remaining 30M become a Tier-2 watchlist, activated only after core penetration. Digital activeness, not raw income, predicts bundle propensity.

Click any barrier for the mechanism that dissolves it

01

Sunk Cost

Financial Pain · #1 · 46%

Annual prepaid fees, router deposits and advance rentals are forfeited the moment they switch. "I'll lose the money I've already paid."

“sunk cost is real. paid 4000 for router deposit. wont lose my deposit.” Counter → Altura Unlock. A Contract Buyout Credit up to ₹5,000 (bill credit, proof-of-loss) plus deposit-free, install-free entry pays off the money already spent — switching starts at zero. See Tab 03.
02

Transition Headache

Execution Pain · 95.2% agree

Days of downtime, router returns and fresh installs. 95.2% say the effort of switching isn't worth the hassle.

“changing setup takes whole day. do the work for me and i will switch.” Counter → Altura Switch Squad. Zero-downtime overlap, reverse logistics, one-KYC-once and letter-of-authority cancellation — Altura does the switching so the household does nothing.
03

Reliability Risk — Single Point of Failure

Operational Pain · 69.2%

One provider = one point of failure. 69.2% intentionally fragment for backup — an outage would take down calls, WiFi, TV and payments at once.

“wfh means i need 99.9 uptime. if airtel network is down i have no upi.” Counter → Altura Fail-Safe. Fibre⇄5G auto-failover, reciprocal VoWiFi and a published Household Outage Score turn the single-provider risk into the single-provider advantage.
04

Loss of Choice

Hold-up Pain · 56.8%

Give one provider everything and lose all leverage. 56.8% believe consolidation leads to price abuse — "loyal customers get the worst deals."

“they'll raise prices once they have me.” Counter → Altura Charter. A published one-tap exit ramp, a 24-month price lock at 3+ services and cancel-any-one-keep-the-rest. Freedom to leave is exactly what makes them stay.
Barrier → mechanism, one-to-one: Sunk Cost → Unlock · Transition → Switch Squad · SPOF → Fail-Safe · Loss of Choice → Charter
